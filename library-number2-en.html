Counting numbers

Practice counting by tens and hundredths

Part 1: Fill in the missing numbers in the blanks.

  • The counter variable increases by 1 unit.
  • QS1: 1 , 2,___, 4,___, 6
  • QS2: 32, 33 ,___, 35,___, 37
  • QS3: 99, 100,___, 102,___, 104
  • The counter variable increases by 5 unit.
  • QS4: 5, ___, 15, ___, 25, ___
  • QS5: 40, 45, ___, 55, ___, 65
  • QS6: 25, 30, ___, 40, ___, 50
  • The counter variable increases by 10 unit.
  • QS7: 10, ___, 30, ___, 50, ___, 70
  • QS8: 10, 20, ___, 40, ___, 60, ___
  • QS9: 90, 80, ___, ___, 50, ___
  • The counter variable increases by 20 unit.
  • QS10: 5, 25, ___, 65, ___, 105
  • QS11: 10, 30, ___, 70, ___, 110
  • QS12: 0, 20, ___, 60, ___, 100

Part 2: Write and arrange the numbers in order

  • *In ascending order
  • QS1:50 – 10 – 70 – 30 – 90
  • QS2: 47 – 32 – 58 – 21 – 65
  • QS3: 28 – 14 – 35 – 21 – 7
  • *In descending order
  • QS4:80 – 20 – 60 – 40 - 100 - 120
  • QS5: 90 – 45 – 75 – 60 – 30 - 105
  • QS6:100 – 75 – 150 – 120 – 90

Part 3: Write the number between two numbers

  • QS1:7 and 12
  • QS2: 10 and 15
  • QS3: 20 and 24
  • QS4:54 and 60
  • QS5:18 and 23
  • QS6:38 and 45
  • QS7:49 and 51
  • QS8:63 and 67
  • QS9:75 and 80

Part 4: Answer the questions

  • QS1: 3 cats went out and met 4 other cats. How many cats are there now?
  • QS2: There are 5 apples on the table, the baby ate 2. How many are left?
  • QS3: There are 7 chickens, 3 are gone. How many are left?
  • QS4: There are 2 fish in the pond, plus 3 more. What is the total number of fish?
  • QS5: The baby has 4 cars, you give him 2 more cars. What is the total number of cars?
